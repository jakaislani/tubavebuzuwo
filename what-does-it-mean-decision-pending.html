Does decision in process mean rejection?

What does decision in process mean?

Answer: When a journal displays the “decision in process” status, it means that the editorial board is in the process of making the final decision about your paper. … There’s no specific time frame for this; the time may vary depending on the journal and the field.

What is pending approval in journal?

Pending approval – The editor is due to make a decision on your manuscript.

What does pending recommendation mean?

Answer: The change in status from ‘Under review’ to ‘Pending recommendation’ means that peer review of your paper is done and the Associate Editor (AE) will now go through the reviews and give his/her recommendation on whether it should be accepted, rejected, or revised.

How long it takes decision in process?

Answer: Most journals normally take 4-8 weeks to review manuscripts. The ‘decision in process’ status implies that your manuscript has passed through peer review and the editorial board is now making a decision.

How long does the decision process take?

The duration of decision making may range from a month to four years, but usually takes about 12 months. Most processes run into disruptions and interruptions, which lengthen the time taken. Measuring decision making is difficult since it is virtually impossible to define the beginning and end of the process.

What does ready for decision mean?

Answer: When the status for a manuscript changes “Under Review” to “Ready for Decision”, it means that the peer-review process is complete and only the editorial decision is pending.

How long is PNAS review?

On average, a full review takes just 45 days, and most articles publish within 6 months of submission.
